Most "AI for infrastructure" is a chat box bolted onto a docs search. You ask a question, it
hallucinates a plausible answer about *some* cluster, and if you're unlucky it offers to run a command.
We wanted the opposite: an assistant that only ever speaks from **your** state, that learns **your**
conventions, and that is structurally incapable of applying a change on its own.

That assistant is **Elench**, and it's built into the Console. Here's what it is.

## It answers from your real state

Open Elench with **Ask AI** and ask *"Are my connectors healthy?"* or *"Build a dashboard of my
infrastructure."* It doesn't guess — it calls the same org-scoped, permission-checked reads the rest of
the Console uses, and it renders the result as **widgets**: stat cards, tables, charts, pinned onto a
grid next to the chat. Ask for a whole dashboard and it composes one from live data. Click an empty tile
and describe what goes there, and it fills exactly that tile.

Nothing it shows you is invented. If it can't read something, it says so.

## It learns your project — the way a good teammate would

This is the part borrowed, unashamedly, from how modern AI "projects" work. Elench has two kinds of
**workspace**:

- an **Organization** chat — the general assistant for your whole account, and
- a **Project** workspace — scoped to one infrastructure project.

Step into a project and Elench changes with you: it sees only that project's chats, and it picks up that
project's **knowledge base** — the custom instructions and named documents you've pinned. *"This is
production — always require approval before an apply. We drain nodes first. On-call is @platform."* That
context rides every message in the project, layered under your organization-wide policy. A project's
knowledge never leaks into another project, or into the general chat.

You don't have to write down what Elench can already see. Inside a project it also grounds itself on the
live facts it derives on its own — the project's environments and their status, its recent jobs and their
outcomes — so the first answer is already informed, before it calls a single tool. The knowledge panel
shows you exactly that split: *here's what I already know; here's the space for what only you can tell
me.*

## Save what's useful

A dashboard lives in one conversation. Save it as an **artifact** and it becomes a named, org-shared board
your whole team can reopen, view, and drop into any chat — and because each widget stays tied to its
source, a saved board refreshes against live data when you open it. It's a living view, not a screenshot.

## The line that makes it safe

Here's the part we won't compromise on:

> **The deterministic engine is the verdict. The model only proposes.**

Elench can draft a change, but it has **no write path of its own**. A proposed change is re-run through
[Alethia's verification gate](/docs/elench) between `tofu plan` and `tofu apply`, and a real apply is
human-gated. The model is never the thing that decides your infrastructure is safe to
change — a pure, reproducible engine is, and a person clicks the button. Turning on "Auto" mode removes
the per-change confirmation *inside the chat*; it does not, and cannot, bypass that gate.

That's the whole design in a sentence: **an assistant grounded enough to be useful, and constrained
enough to trust.** It reads everything it's allowed to, learns what you teach it, and proposes freely —
because something that isn't a language model is holding the line on what actually ships.

Elench is on now for every Alethia account with AI configured. Open the Console, click **Ask AI**, and
ask it about your infrastructure.
