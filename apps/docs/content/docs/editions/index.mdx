# Editions

Alethia is **open-core**. There are two editions, built from the *same codebase*:

- <Community>Community</Community> — the free, **open-source** edition, licensed **AGPL-3.0**. The complete
  single-tenant product. You can run it for yourself or your team forever, at no cost, with no license key.
- <Enterprise>Enterprise</Enterprise> — the **licensed** edition (commercial). Everything in Community **plus**
  multi-member organizations, teams, SSO, custom roles, the fine-grained access portal, the OpenFGA policy
  engine, and activity export.

<Callout type="info">
  You can **self-host either edition.** "Self-hosting" is about *where Alethia runs* (your own
  infrastructure); "edition" is about *which features are licensed*. Community needs nothing extra; Enterprise
  needs a license key and a couple of extra services (see [Licensing](/editions/licensing)).
</Callout>

## The dividing line

The simplest way to think about it:

> **Everything you need to run Alethia for yourself, or for a team that shares one account, is free and
> open-source. The Enterprise edition is about *many people, many identities* — organizations with multiple
> members, the teams and roles to organize them, the SSO to let them in, and the engine to authorize them at
> scale.**

Concretely:

<Cards>
  <Card title="Community — the whole product, one tenant" href="/editions/feature-matrix">
    The Policy Decision Point, built-in roles, per-resource grants with Org → Project inheritance,
    Row-Level Security tenant isolation, and the authorization Activity log — all open-source. Your account is a
    single-owner "personal organization".
  </Card>
  <Card title="Enterprise — many people, many identities" href="/editions/feature-matrix">
    Multi-member organizations, teams, member invitations, SSO (OIDC + SAML), configurable custom roles, the
    AWS-IAM-style access portal (allow/deny, per-resource), the OpenFGA fine-grained engine, and activity export.
  </Card>
</Cards>

## Why open-core?

The authorization *model* — how a decision is made — is the same in both editions, and it's open. What
Enterprise adds is the machinery for **organizations of people**: ways to invite and group them (members,
teams, invitations), authenticate them (SSO), describe what they can do (custom roles, allow/deny), and decide
it quickly at scale (OpenFGA). None of that is needed to run Alethia for a single tenant — so it isn't in the
free core.

The boundary is enforced in code: **the open-source core never depends on the Enterprise package, and runs
completely without it present.** See the [open-core architecture](/access-control/open-core) for how that's
guaranteed (a one-way dependency + CI guards), and [Licensing](/editions/licensing) for how a license turns
the Enterprise features on.
