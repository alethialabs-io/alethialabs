# Activity

Every Alethia deployment keeps a full **Activity log**, browsable in **Settings → Activity**. This is
<Community>open-source</Community> — it works in the free edition.

## What it records

The Activity log is a **complete record of what happens in the org**. The Policy Decision Point writes a row for:

- **Every action** — create, edit, deploy, destroy, the `manage_*` operations (identities, members, integrations,
  billing), exports, and more.
- **Every denied authorization** — someone tried something they weren't allowed to do.
- **Governance changes** — custom roles created/edited/deleted, access grants assigned/revoked, and member
  lifecycle (joins, removals, role changes).

Successful **reads** (viewing a page or listing resources) are not recorded — they fire constantly and would
drown out the signal; their *denials* still are.

Each entry reads as a natural-language line: the **actor**, what they did, the **resource**, and **when**. Denied
attempts are flagged. Filter by time range, user, project, and event type.

<Callout type="info">
  The log is engine-agnostic: whether the deployment uses the Postgres engine or the
  [OpenFGA engine](/access-control/openfga), decisions are recorded the same way, so Activity looks
  identical.
</Callout>

## Export

<Enterprise />

Exporting the log to **CSV** (for a SIEM, a spreadsheet, or a compliance archive) is an Enterprise feature,
gated by the `activityExport` entitlement. The **Export CSV** button appears in the header when the entitlement is
held; the export is also enforced server-side.
