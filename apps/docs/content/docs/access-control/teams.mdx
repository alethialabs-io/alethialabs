# Teams

<Enterprise />

A **team** is a named group of organization members. Grant access to a team, and **everyone on it inherits the
grant** — add or remove people from the team and their access follows automatically.

## Why teams

Without teams, sharing a project with five engineers means five grants — and five revokes when the work ends.
With a team, it's **one grant to the team**, and membership is the only thing you manage afterward.

Teams are the natural principal for durable, role-based access: "the platform team can deploy the
production projects" is one grant on one team.

## Managing teams

In **Settings → Teams**:

<Steps>
<Step>**New team** — give it a name (e.g. `platform`).</Step>
<Step>**Manage** — add or remove organization members.</Step>
<Step>Grant access to the team in the [Access portal](/access-portal) — pick the team as the principal.</Step>
</Steps>

## How team grants resolve

A grant whose principal is a team applies to a user whenever they're a member of that team. This is resolved in
**both** authorization engines:

- The Postgres engine matches team grants by joining your team memberships at decision time.
- The OpenFGA engine models a team as a group (`team:… # member`), so a team grant reaches members through the
  relationship graph.

Either way, removing someone from a team **immediately** removes the access that came from it.

<Callout type="info">
  Teams compose with everything else: grant a [custom role](/access-control/custom-roles) to a team, scope it
  to a [project](/access-control/grants-and-scopes), and add per-member [deny](/access-control/access-portal)
  exceptions on top.
</Callout>
