# Members & invitations

<Enterprise />

In the open-source <Community>Community</Community> edition your organization has exactly one member: you, the
owner. The <Enterprise>Enterprise</Enterprise> edition adds **multiple members** and the flow to bring them in.

## The invitation lifecycle

<Steps>
<Step>
### Invite
In **Settings → Members → Invite member**, enter an email and pick a role. An invitation is created and an
email is sent with an accept link.
</Step>
<Step>
### Accept
The invitee clicks the link and lands on the accept page. If they're not signed in, they're sent to sign-in
first and returned afterward — and because email sign-in doubles as sign-up, this works even if they don't have
an account yet.
</Step>
<Step>
### Join
Accepting creates their **membership** in the organization with the chosen role.
</Step>
<Step>
### Authorize
Membership is turned into real authorization: the member's role is synced into a **grant**, so the
[Policy Decision Point](/access-control/architecture) starts authorizing them immediately.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  The last step matters. An organization's membership role and the authorization **grant** are kept in step
  automatically — adding a member, changing their role, or removing them updates their access without any
  manual grant management.
</Callout>

## Roles for members

When you invite or edit a member you pick one of the [roles](/access-control/roles-and-permissions) — the four
built-ins or a [custom role](/access-control/custom-roles). For finer control (scoped or deny grants), use the
[Access portal](/access-portal) after they've joined.

## Switching organizations

A member can belong to more than one organization and switch between them with the **organization switcher** in
the dashboard header. The active organization scopes everything — what you see, and what the PDP and Row-Level
Security enforce.
