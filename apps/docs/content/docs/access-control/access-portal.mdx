# The Access portal

<Enterprise />

**Settings → Access** is where you assign access, the way you'd attach policies in the AWS IAM portal. It shows
every grant in the organization and lets you create new ones.

## Granting access

Click **Grant access** and fill in the four parts of a [grant](/access-control/grants-and-scopes):

<Steps>
<Step>
### Who
Pick a **member** or a **team** <Enterprise />. A team grant reaches everyone on the team.
</Step>
<Step>
### Allow or Deny
**Allow** confers access; **Deny** is an explicit exception that overrides any inherited allow.
</Step>
<Step>
### What
Choose a **Role** (built-in or [custom](/access-control/custom-roles)) **or** a **single permission**
(e.g. `spec:deploy`).
</Step>
<Step>
### Scope
**Entire organization**, or a specific **zone / spec / runner / cloud identity**. A grant on a container flows
down to its children.
</Step>
</Steps>

The grants table lists each entry with its **principal**, **effect** (Allow/Deny), **role or permission**, and
**scope**, and a button to revoke it.

## The deny-exception workflow

The portal is where you build the canonical exception — broad access with a precise carve-out:

<Steps>
<Step>Grant **Allow · viewer · zone `Z`** to Dana.</Step>
<Step>Grant **Deny · `spec:view` · spec `S`** to Dana.</Step>
<Step>Dana sees `Z` and its other specs, but not `S`.</Step>
</Steps>

See [grants & scopes](/access-control/grants-and-scopes) for the precedence rules behind this.

## What you can pick from

- **Principals** — the org's members and teams.
- **Roles** — the four built-ins plus your [custom roles](/access-control/custom-roles).
- **Permissions** — the full `resource:action` catalog for single-permission grants.
- **Resources** — your zones, specs, runners, and cloud identities, for scoped grants.

<Callout type="info">
  In the open-source <Community>Community</Community> edition there's a single owner who already holds every
  permission, so there's nothing to assign — the Access portal is an Enterprise surface.
</Callout>
