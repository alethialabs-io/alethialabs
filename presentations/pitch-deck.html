The Problem

Getting to production forces a bad trade.

Every team building on Kubernetes hits the same wall — and every escape route costs ownership, security, or weeks.

01

The platform-eng tax

A real cluster — networking, databases, caching, DNS, secrets, registries, autoscaling, GitOps — is weeks of Terraform/Helm/YAML. Every team rebuilds the same wheel.

02

Rent a black box

The PaaS that saves the weeks — Porter, Qovery, Northflank — runs your control plane on their servers and locks deploys into their abstraction. Leaving means re-platforming.

03

Credential leakage

Both paths leak long-lived cloud keys and admin kubeconfigs into CI and third-party SaaS — the thing every audit flags.

DIY keeps ownership but brings back the tax. Rent removes the tax but takes your keys and your control plane. No option keeps both.

The Solution · The Anti-Porter

Alethia gives you the real thing — and you own all of it.

One Spec → a remote runner provisions a complete platform in your cloud account, assuming roles at runtime so no credentials are ever stored, and hands you a self-managing, GitOps-wired cluster.

Same outcome as the rented PaaS — a production app platform, fast. Opposite ownership model. It's your cluster, in your account, with standard tools you can take anywhere. Alethia never sits in the data path.

Zero-trust provisioning

Runner assumes cloud roles at execution time. Control plane never sees or stores your keys.

Own it / self-hostable

You own the cluster — and can self-host Alethia itself as ~4 containers. No SaaS dependency.

GitOps app-delivery

Standard ArgoCD wired to your repo. Not a proprietary pipeline you can't leave.

Pluggable + multi-cloud

Bring your own tools per category, across many clouds.

Why Now

The ownership backlash meets a mature stack.

Trust has flipped

Post-breach, post-audit, teams refuse to hand cloud keys to a hosted control plane. Zero-trust and "own your stack" are now buying criteria, not nice-to-haves.

The primitives are standard

EKS/GKE/AKS, ArgoCD, external-secrets, Karpenter, OpenTofu — the building blocks are mature and portable. The missing piece is the wiring, not the parts.

Open-core is proven

Cal.com, GitLab, PostHog, Plausible validated AGPL-core + commercial ee/ + hosted SaaS. The model and the playbook now exist.

License repricing

HashiCorp's BSL move and the OpenTofu fork pushed the market toward truly open IaC. Alethia generates real OpenTofu you own.

Sovereignty pressure

EU data-residency and air-gapped requirements reward a self-hostable control plane and cheaper/EU-native cloud breadth.

AI raises the bar

Repo-to-Spec scanning and MCP make guided design realistic — a future premium meter on top of the free core.

Unit economics · why margins are structurally high

~90–99% software gross margin (est.) — the customer's cluster (EKS / Aurora / nodes) runs in their cloud account, so they pay that bill, not us. Our COGS is just the fixed multi-tenant control plane + a few cents of runner-minutes per provisioning op ≈ ~$3–5 / customer / month at scale.

Free self-hosters pay $0; revenue = hosting + ee/ + usage + support — never the open-source core. Model: Cal.com (AGPL core + ee/) + a runner-minutes meter Cal.com lacks. All $ figures are estimates / proposed.
