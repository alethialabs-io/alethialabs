Executive Summary
Confidential · 2026
Company

Alethia provisions a complete, GitOps-wired Kubernetes platform on your cloud account — with zero stored credentials — and hands you a cluster you own. From one Spec, a remote runner stands up EKS + VPC + Aurora + ElastiCache + DynamoDB + SQS/SNS + ECR + S3 + Secrets Manager + Route 53 + WAF, installs ArgoCD wired to your Git repo with auto-sync, layers in a real operator suite, and hands over kubeconfig, ArgoCD URL, and every endpoint. Your apps deploy from a git push. It's your cluster, in your account — Alethia never sits in the data path.

The Problem

Getting from "we have an app" to "it runs in production on Kubernetes" forces a bad trade. A real production cluster — networking, databases, caching, DNS, secrets, registries, autoscaling, GitOps — is weeks of Terraform, Helm, and YAML that every team rebuilds from scratch. That's the platform-engineering tax.

The PaaS tools that save you those weeks — Porter, Qovery, Northflank — make you hand them your cloud keys, run your control plane on their servers, and lock your deploys into their black box. The DIY route keeps ownership but brings the tax back. Both paths leak long-lived cloud keys and admin kubeconfigs into CI and third-party SaaS — the exact thing every security audit flags.

The Solution — the anti-Porter

Alethia gives you the real, standard thing — EKS + ArgoCD + your own tools — provisioned for you, that you own end to end. Porter/Qovery/Northflank rent you a proprietary PaaS that holds your keys and runs your control plane. Alethia delivers the same outcome — a production app platform, fast — with the opposite ownership model. Four pillars set it apart:

01

Zero-trust remote provisioning

The runner runs in your account and assumes cloud roles at execution time. The control plane never sees or stores your keys — short-lived, scoped, nothing to leak.

02

Own it / self-hostable

You own the cluster — and you can self-host the Alethia control plane itself as ~4 containers (Postgres + S3 + app + runner). No Supabase, no SaaS dependency. Run it air-gapped, in-region.

03

GitOps app-delivery

Standard ArgoCD wired to your repo with auto-sync (Kustomize/Helm) — not a proprietary pipeline. There's nothing to leave: it's your cluster, your ArgoCD, your repo.

04

Pluggable integrations + multi-cloud

Bring your own tools per category — Cloudflare DNS, Vault, Datadog/Grafana/Prometheus, Docker Hub — across many clouds. No opinionated-stack lock-in.

Market

Business Model — open-core

AGPL-3.0 core (provisioning + GitOps + integrations + single-tenant + community RBAC) is free and self-hostable — a model closely mirroring Cal.com. The commercial ee/ tier adds orgs, RBAC-at-scale, SSO (SAML/OIDC), audit, and multi-tenancy. Three revenue lines:

Primary

Hosted Alethia Cloud

Multi-tenant SaaS — zero ops, managed Postgres/S3, managed runner fleet, SSO/RBAC/audit by tier. The dominant line for an AGPL company.

Contract

Self-managed Enterprise

Teams that must self-host (sovereignty, air-gapped, AGPL-averse) but need governance run ee/ on-prem under commercial license + support/SLA.

Meter

Usage-based

AI premium (repo-scanner / MCP) and cloud-hosted runner-minutes for the runner fleet. Stacks on top of seats.

~90–99% software gross margin. The customer's cluster (EKS/Aurora/etc.) runs in their cloud account — they pay that bill, not us. Alethia's COGS is just the control plane (fixed, amortized multi-tenant) plus runner-minutes (cents per provisioning op) ≈ ~$3–5 / customer / month at scale. Capital-efficient by design: open-source distribution (low CAC) × top-decile margin. ($ figures are estimates / proposed.)

Tiers: Community (free, AGPL, self-host the full core) · Team (~$299/mo, hosted) · Business (~$999/mo, hosted; orgs/teams, RBAC, audit) · Enterprise (from ~$2,500/mo, annual; ee/ SSO/SAML, full audit, multi-tenancy, SLA; self-managed license option). Add-ons: runner-minutes (metered) · AI premium (per-scan, metered). Free self-hosters pay $0 — revenue = hosting + ee/ + usage + support, never the open-source core.
